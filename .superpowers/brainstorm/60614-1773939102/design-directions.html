Two Evolution Approaches

How far to push the visual refresh — both optimize for light + dark, athletic premium, and earned color

A

Surgical Polish

Philosophy: Keep the current structure, elevate the craft. No new screens, no layout changes — just make every existing surface feel intentional.


What changes:

  • Unify typography — migrate all raw .font(.system(...)) to design tokens
  • Pull emerald back to meaningful moments (CTAs, progress, active states)
  • Add entrance animations to main screens (staggered reveals like onboarding)
  • Introduce subtle card hierarchy — not all cards are equal
  • Consolidate corner radius tokens (kill the legacy/v1.1 overlap)
  • Add haptic feedback to key interactions (set completion, workout finish)
  • Refine Coach tab: better visual hierarchy, hero moment at top

~2-3 days of work. Low risk. Immediately noticeable improvement.

B

Brand Evolution

Philosophy: Everything in Option A, plus introduce new visual vocabulary that makes Povver unmistakably Povver. The app gets a signature look.


What changes (in addition to A):

  • New Coach tab hero — status-driven landing with coach presence indicator
  • Signature metric cards — progress rings, trend sparklines, earned-color data viz
  • Workout execution gets a "performance mode" feel — tighter, more focused
  • Section headers get the Whoop treatment — uppercase micro labels, strong hierarchy
  • Introduce a subtle brand texture or gradient moment (not grain — something lighter, for both modes)
  • Library/History get proper information density — more like Oura's detail screens
  • Post-workout summary screen becomes a celebration moment
  • Motion language: consistent spring curves, meaningful transitions between states

~5-7 days of work. Medium risk. Transforms the feel of the app.

My Recommendation: B

Option A fixes inconsistencies but doesn't close the gap you're feeling. The onboarding set a bar that "just polish" won't reach — the main app needs its own visual identity, not just cleaner versions of what's there. Option B gives the app a signature while keeping the usability intact. The key is that every new visual element earns its place through meaning (data, state, achievement), not decoration.
